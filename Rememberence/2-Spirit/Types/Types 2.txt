Types of Souls

#) Type; [Materia]

As we are mind, so too we are the forces of nature, the matter that the mind perceives.

------------------------------------
When an item or entity is created, it starts with a number of traits equal to its current tier level and may never have a number of traits beyond its tier level. For instance, a weapon crafted at the Novice tier would have 1 trait. As the item or entity progresses through tiers, it gains an additional trait for each tier level it advances. These may be chosen at random for NPCs with a d6. The same trait can be taken more than once, multiplying the effcts of that trait. Active traits require an amount of MP to activate equal to what ever Biorhythm is controlling the effect, while passive effects are always on if their conditions are met.

1) Thunder; [Voltara]

The storm is within your soul, and the lightning surges fourth from your intentions. Thunder type spirits have the storm woven into their being, and resonate with all things electric. They are deeper thinkers about the vibrational nature of all things large and very small. They may discover they are quick to find the root of situations, although this sometimes makes them jump to conclusions. Quick thinking and snap judgements are characteristic of Thunder spirits. Active and energetic, they tend to be the life of the party. Whether that is through shocking displays of stupidity, or a sudden burst of profound inspiration, it all depends on who is wielding them.

Thunder spirits embody the themes of intensity, sudden insight, and the electrifying energy of storms, reflecting an association with transformation, depth, and powerful emotions. They represent the quick, often intuitive understanding of situations, the electric surge of inspiration or action, and the dual nature of being both profoundly insightful and impulsively reactive. Thunder spirits symbolize the storm within, the sudden flashes of brilliance or folly, teaching about the power of quick thinking, the importance of depth in perception, and the balance between intuition and rash judgment.

	Society: Thunder spirits are seen as symbols of sudden change, enlightenment, and the balance between wisdom and impulsivity. Their presence in society often signifies moments of transformation or the need for quick, decisive action.

	Events: They are central to myths and Events involving sudden revelations or the dual nature of lightning - both destructive and illuminating.
Habitat: Preferring environments charged with energy, like storm clouds or areas with electrical phenomena, they thrive where the air crackles with potential.

	Habitat: Preferring environments charged with energy, like storm clouds or areas with electrical phenomena, they thrive where the air crackles with potential.

	Narrative:  Encounters with Thunder spirits might prompt players to make quick decisions, offer sudden insights, or serve as a caution against rash actions, emphasizing themes of intuition, depth, and the balance between action and thought.

2) Warrior; [Warraga]

Raising your weapon to the enemy, you shall not be tamed! By no man, nor beast, nor god! Warriors bring with them a fierce battle instinct, and a strong arm to any situation. Warrior spirits are strict negotiators, just as much as any spirit who fights on the battlefield. The warrior is a master of confrontation, not always aiming to slaughter their opponent, but to become stronger along with them, if the situation calls for it. While having the capacity to easily overtake anyone who stands in the way of their pursuits. They have mastered the capacity for physical and verbal force, while knowing when and where to apply it.

Warrior spirits embody a steadfast, determined, and sometimes stubborn mentality, known for their unyielding resolve in battle and negotiation. They represent persistence and strength, not just in physical combat but in all forms of confrontation. Warriors are not just fighters; they are also strategists and diplomats, using force or words as needed, embodying the balance between power and restraint.

	Society: Warriors are icons of strength, resilience, and direct confrontation, often revered for their ability to face challenges head-on, embodying the spirit of determination and practical problem-solving.

	Events: Celebrated in stories of heroism, their trials and triumphs are central to cultural narratives, often depicted in rites that test both physical prowess and mental fortitude.

	Habitat: Preferring environments where their skills are tested, like battlefields or strategic locations, they thrive in scenarios that demand strength and tactical acumen.

	Narrative: Meeting a Warrior spirit might prompt players to confront their own challenges directly, learn the art of negotiation through strength, or understand the balance between aggression and restraint, teaching lessons in perseverance, strategic thinking, and personal growth through adversity.

3) Spellcaster; [Cultia]

Sorceries are your specialty, to help or hinder is a matter of who's taking advantage. Verbal and mental mastery is the domain of the Spellcaster, who speak their truth into being. Using various runes and compounds to create powerful magics. They have a profound thirst for knowledge, and will seek it at all costs. They tend to hoard their knowledge for fear that others may use it unwisely. It is not uncommon for Spellcasters to work with words as librarians or teachers, often preferring to be with books over people. Though they often keep to themselves, they are powerful beyond comprehension when they unleash the mysteries they hold.

Spellcaster spirits embody the themes of knowledge, mastery over magic, and the ethical considerations of power, reflecting traits of meticulousness, service, and a quest for perfection. They represent the power of spoken and mental mastery, using their understanding of the universe to craft spells and enchantments. Spellcasters are driven by an insatiable thirst for knowledge, often hoarding it due to fears of misuse, symbolizing the dual nature of knowledge as both a tool for good and a potential for harm.

	Society: Spellcasters are revered for their magical prowess and knowledge, often seen as keepers of ancient secrets, embodying the balance between power and responsibility.

	Events: Central to tales of magical quests, ethical dilemmas, or the pursuit of forbidden knowledge, their stories often explore themes of power, wisdom, and isolation.

	Habitat: They are found in environments conducive to study and solitude, like arcane libraries or secluded towers, where they can delve deep into their research.

	Narrative: Encounters with Spellcasters might prompt players to seek knowledge, confront the ethical use of power, or learn about the perils of isolation, teaching about the value of wisdom, the dangers of unchecked power, and the importance of sharing knowledge.

4) Pyro; [Plasmara]

Calling the inner fire, and the furious passion that comes with it. Fire, fire, burning brighter, the Pyro have a nearly unhealthy fascination with flames. Whether that's the light of a candle burning subtly, or a wildfire raging brazenly. They are excitable and energetic spirits, who tend to burst with emotion at times where it may be unwarranted. Though if they can tame their flame, they can spark a fire in even the coldest heart with their passion. The brightest blaze burns half as long, and they must keep their cool, or risk burning out too quickly.

Pyro spirits embody fiery passion, energy, and sometimes uncontrollable nature. They represent the element of fire in all its forms, from the gentle flicker of a candle to the wild, destructive force of a forest fire. Pyro spirits are characterized by their intense emotions, enthusiasm, and the potential for both creation and destruction. They symbolize the dual nature of passion - its ability to inspire and its risk of burning out if not managed.

	Society: Pyros are symbols of passion, transformation, and the raw energy of fire, often seen as leaders or catalysts for change, embodying the spirit of enthusiasm.

	Events: Central to narratives about emotional intensity, transformation through fire, or the dangers of uncontrolled passion, their stories often explore themes of self-control and the power of fire.

	Habitat: They thrive in environments where fire is a natural element, like volcanic areas or deserts, or where fire Events are common, reflecting their deep connection to fire.

	Narrative: Encounters with Pyro spirits might prompt players to ignite their own passion, learn to manage intense emotions, or understand the balance between fire's creative and destructive forces, teaching about the power of passion, the importance of self-regulation, and the transformative nature of fire.

5) Plant; [Herbalia]

Like the blossoming forests, your plants grow with the full force of nature. Plant type spirits have a tendency to build foundations, taking root, then growing over large areas by spreading seeds. Growing through broken cities, and ancient ruins, the breath of nature at it's most simplistic. The first plant spirits who emerged from the primordial chaos would float on top of the waters and spread their leaves. Plant type spirits represent natural growth in both it's beauty, and capacity for destruction. Signifiers of the end of decay, and a resurgence of growth.

Plant spirits embody the themes of foundation, growth, and the cyclical nature of life, reflecting an association with structure, ambition, and the enduring qualities of nature. They represent the power of natural expansion, the beauty and resilience of life, and the inevitable cycle of decay and rebirth. Plant spirits symbolize the enduring spirit of nature, the slow but unstoppable force of growth, and the balance between creation and destruction, teaching about patience, the value of foundations, and the natural order of life's cycles.

	Society: Plants are revered for their ability to endure, adapt, and thrive, embodying themes of patience, structural integrity, and the cyclical nature of existence.

	Events: Central to tales of ecological renewal, the balance of nature, or the slow but inevitable march of life, their stories often explore themes of rebirth, resilience, and the natural order.

	Habitat: They are found where nature is reclaiming or has reclaimed land, from ancient ruins to vibrant forests, reflecting their role in ecological renewal.

	Narrative: Encounters with Plant spirits might prompt players to reflect on the importance of patience, the strength found in foundations, or the acceptance of life's cycles, teaching about the enduring power of nature, the slow progress of growth, and the balance between life and decay.

6) Fairy; [Powdara]

From here or there? From to or frow? The secrets shared, the fairies know. Beings from between worlds, the fairies have gained access to ancient wisdom that extends beyond the reach of time. They carry with them insight, be it in the form of catastrophic mischief, or in the form of riddles and poems. Those drawn to the fairy have an inclination toward pulling pranks, and getting away with it. They may also take objects out of phase, making them effectively disappear for days, weeks, or even years. Only to reappear as if they had just vanished moments ago. Mysterious beings that strive to make life more interesting for anyone they meet.

Fairy spirits embody freedom, whimsy, and interworld connections, reflecting themes of exploration and knowledge. They symbolize ancient wisdom, playful magic, and transcending boundaries, teaching about curiosity, life's mysteries, and the interconnectedness of all things.

	Society: Fairies are revered for their freedom, their connections to otherworldly knowledge, and their playful nature, embodying the spirit of adventure and curiosity.

	Events: Central to tales of exploration, the uncovering of hidden truths, or the playful side of magic, their stories often explore themes of discovery, the joy of learning, and the interconnectedness of all things.

	Habitat: They are found in places where reality is thin, like mystical forests or enchanted gardens, reflecting their role as bridge-makers between worlds.

	Narrative: Encounters with Fairy spirits might prompt players to embrace the unknown, find joy in exploration, or learn through playful or mysterious means, teaching about the value of curiosity, the unexpected beauty in life, and the web of connections that binds all things.

7) Beast; [Creatura]

Thoughts are primal, like the beasts of the jungle and plains. A vicious hunter, never missing their kill. Beast type spirits are those who act as if on instinct. Whether they are prey or predator, they think through action. All animals who lack higher thinking are considered beast type. Spirits who have lost their higher reasoning, by some magic, or toxin, may also be considered beast type. Mindless animals who act more on stimulus than on thought. Everything from rabbits to lions are beasts we've known. They survive with the most blatant expression of their nature, because that's all they've got.

Beast spirits embody the primal, instinct-driven nature often associated with earthy and sometimes stubborn qualities. They represent raw survival instincts, the essence of nature's unfiltered expressions of life. Beasts act on instinct, whether as predators or prey, symbolizing the purest form of existence where action precedes thought. This type includes all creatures driven by primal urges, reflecting a life lived through the most basic expressions of survival and instinct.

	Society: Beasts are revered for their strength, their unyielding survival instincts, and their connection to the raw, untamed aspects of nature, embodying the wild essence of life.

	Events: Central to tales of survival, the wild heart of nature, or the transformation into more primal forms, their stories often explore themes of instinct versus intellect, the beauty of raw life, and the balance between civilization and wilderness.

	Habitat: They thrive in environments where survival is a daily challenge, from dense jungles to expansive plains, reflecting their deep connection to the wild.

	Narrative: Encounters with Beast spirits might prompt players to reconnect with their primal instincts, understand the beauty in nature's raw expressions, or face the consequences of losing higher reasoning, teaching about the balance between instinct and intellect, the value of survival skills, and the essence of life's most basic forms.

11) Insect; [Infestia]

Swarming bugs of all kinds, they infest, multiply, and consume. They are instinctive hunger and reproduction run a muck. Thoughtless yet almost mechanically organized, they form either hives, or nests, from which they multiply and spread. If you meet an Insect type spirit, don't let it grow to a catalyst, or it will expand beyond your control. If they grow beyond your awareness, infesting the hidden corners beware, for they came in swarms, and the villagers couldn't respond in time to save their crops, or their herds, or themselves...

Insect spirits embody the relentless, instinctual drive to consume and multiply, symbolizing uncontrolled growth and the raw, mechanical organization of nature's smallest but most numerous creatures. They represent a pioneering spirit, but in a form that's about expansion through sheer numbers rather than individual bravery. These spirits are a reminder of nature's capacity for sudden, overwhelming change.

	Society: Insects are often seen as symbols of rapid, unchecked growth, adaptability, and the primal, less heroic aspects of nature, embodying themes of fertility, chaos, and the balance of life.

	Events: They might feature in Events or stories as warnings against overpopulation or as symbols of nature's fertility gone wild, like in The Swarm's Reckoning.

	Habitat: Found in places where resources are abundant, conducive to their rapid reproduction and expansion, such as forests, fields, or any fertile environment.

	Narrative: Encounters with Insect spirits could symbolize the need for vigilance against small threats, the balance between growth and destruction, or the consequences of ignoring minor issues until they become unmanageable, teaching about the importance of balance, the raw force of nature, and the vigilance required in life.

24) Avatar; [Runaga]

Those who are among the Avatar spirits merge their Types with forces around them. Melding the powers they make to become the most balanced form of themselves, knowing that no other is the same as any other. Those who have a natural intuition about what derives meaning in all things. Consciousness in the flows and patterns of the cosmos themselves. Avatar spirits are those who seek the ultimate balance of chaos and order.

Avatar spirits embody the themes of unity, balance, and cosmic consciousness, reflecting an association with exploration, understanding, and the search for meaning. They represent the fusion of various energies, the quest for equilibrium between chaos and order, and an intuitive grasp of the universe's underlying patterns. Avatars symbolize the journey towards self-realization through understanding the interconnectedness of all elements, teaching about the importance of balance, the exploration of one's inner and outer worlds, and the pursuit of a harmonious existence with the cosmos.

	Society: Avatar spirits symbolize unity, balance, and cosmic consciousness, embodying the quest for understanding and meaning.

Traits;

Active:

	Negative: Duality, if this spirit preforms or receives a Critical Hit/Fail all damage dealt to them is doubled until end of turn.
